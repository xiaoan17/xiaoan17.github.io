Vehicular Speed Prediction Method for Highway Scenarios Based on Spatio-Temporal Graph Convolutional Networks and Potential Field Theory
IEEE Internet of Things Journal IF = 8.2, Q1 2024
Linheng Li, Bocheng An, Dapeng Zhang, Rui Gan, Zhi Zhou, Xu Qu, Bin Ran
📄 IEEE Xplore 🎓 Google Scholar
展开摘要 ▾
Traffic flow analysis largely depends on accurate predictions of microscopic speed. This study constructs a vehicle generalized-dynamic graph to characterize vehicle trajectory, and proposes the Dynamic Edge Graph Convolutional Network (DEGCN). Experimental results on three real-world datasets demonstrate superior prediction performance over baseline models.
A Freeway Traffic Flow Prediction Model Based on a Generalized Dynamic Spatio-Temporal Graph Convolutional Network
IEEE Trans. Intelligent Transportation Systems IF = 7.9, Q1 2024
Rui Gan, Bocheng An, Linheng Li, Xu Qu, Bin Ran
📄 IEEE Xplore 🎓 Google Scholar
展开摘要 ▾
Introduces the Generalized Dynamic Spatio-Temporal Graph Convolutional Network (GDSTGCN) for traffic data, building a learning-based generalized dynamic graph structure that incorporates both spatial and temporal connections and evolves with real-time traffic data.
A Freeway Vehicle Early Warning Method Based on Risk Map: Enhancing Traffic Safety Through Global Perspective Characterization of Driving Risk
Accident Analysis & Prevention IF = 5.7, Q1 2024
Chuang Cui, Bocheng An, Linheng Li, Xu Qu, Huhe Manda, Bin Ran
📄 Elsevier 🎓 Google Scholar
展开摘要 ▾
Proposes a freeway vehicle early warning method based on risk map using Modified Time-to-Collision (MTTC). Simulation results show total warning time reduced by 29.6% and 73.3% of vehicles change lanes away from the accident vehicle.
Stability Analysis and Numerical Simulation of a Car-Following Model Considering Safety Potential Field and V2X Communication

Linheng Li, Bocheng An, Zhiyu Wang, Jing Gan, Xu Qu, Bin Ran
📄 Elsevier 🎓 Google Scholar

Develops an improved car-following model considering effects of multiple leading vehicles under V2X environment, constructing distance-based and field-based models for influence weights. The field-based model outperforms the distance-based model.
